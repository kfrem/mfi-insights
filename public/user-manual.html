Section 1 — Introduction

What is MFI Clarity?

MFI Clarity is a complete Microfinance Management System designed specifically for Ghana's microfinance sector. It replaces paper-based records and error-prone spreadsheets with a secure, always-online platform that manages every aspect of your microfinance institution — from client registration to Bank of Ghana (BOG) regulatory reporting.

🌐 Access Anywhere

Works in any modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari). No software installation needed. Works on desktop computers, laptops, and tablets.

🔒 Secure by Design

Your data is encrypted and protected. Every user gets their own password. Sensitive data is never shared between organisations.

📋 BOG Compliant

Built to meet Bank of Ghana regulatory requirements. Classification of Advances, CAR, Liquidity, and AML reports are generated automatically.

ℹ️ System Requirements

A modern web browser (Google Chrome recommended) · Stable internet connection · Any device: desktop computer, laptop, or tablet · No software installation required

Section 2 — Getting Started

Registration, Email Verification & Onboarding

Before you can use MFI Clarity, the first person at your organisation must create an account and set up the organisation. This is usually the system administrator or the CEO/MD. All other staff will be added later.

1

Open MFI Clarity in your web browser

Type the MFI Clarity web address in your browser. You will see the Welcome page. Click Get Started or Register. This takes you to the Registration page.

2

Fill in your Registration details

  • Display Name — Your name as it will appear in the system (optional but recommended)
  • Email Address — Must be a real email address you have access to. Example: john.mensah@mymfi.com
  • Password — Choose a password with at least 6 characters. Use letters AND numbers for security
  • Confirm Password — Type your password again exactly the same way

When done, click the Create Account button.

3

Verify your Email Address

Check your email inbox. You will receive an email from MFI Clarity with a verification link. Click that link to confirm your email. If you do not see it, check your Spam/Junk folder. You must verify your email before you can log in.

4

Set Up Your Organisation (Onboarding)

After email verification, you will be taken to the Organisation Setup page. Fill in all your organisation's details:

  • Legal Name (required) — The official registered name of your MFI. Example: Accra Microfinance Limited
  • Trading Name — The name you trade as (if different from legal name)
  • Registration Number — Your company registration number from the Registrar General's Department
  • Tax ID (TIN) — Your Tax Identification Number from GRA
  • Street Address — Your office physical address
  • City/Town — The town or city where your office is located
  • Region — Select from the dropdown (all 16 Ghana regions listed)
  • Phone Number — Your main contact phone number
  • Email Address — Your organisation's contact email
  • Website — Optional: your website address

Click Create Organisation when done. You will be automatically assigned the ADMIN role.

5

You Are Now Inside MFI Clarity!

After setup, you will land on the Executive Dashboard — the main home screen. From here you can navigate to any part of the system using the sidebar menu on the left.

⚠️ Forgot Your Password?

On the Login page, click "Forgot Password". Enter your email address and click Send Reset Link. Check your email for a password reset link and follow the instructions. Your old password will no longer work after you set a new one.

Section 3 — Navigation Overview

Finding Your Way Around MFI Clarity

MFI Clarity has a sidebar navigation menu on the left side of the screen. Click on any item to go to that section. The menu items you can see depend on your role (Admins and Managers see the most options).

📌 Sidebar Menu Items

  • Dashboard — Executive KPI overview (home)
  • Board — Board of Directors view
  • Management — Daily operations dashboard
  • Shareholders — Investor portal
  • Departments — Accounts/Collections/Credit reports
  • Financial Reports — Statements, profitability
  • Regulatory Reports — BOG compliance reports
  • Portfolio Aging — Loan overdue analysis
  • Repayments — 60-day collection trends
  • Field Operations — Mobile collections
  • Data Entry — Add clients, loans, payments
  • Audit Log — Activity trail
  • User Management — Staff & roles
  • Settings — Organisation configuration

🔄 Key Interface Elements

  • Organisation Name — Shown at top of sidebar. If you belong to multiple organisations, click to switch between them
  • Refresh Button — Updates dashboard data to the latest figures. Look for the refresh icon on dashboards
  • Export Buttons — Most tables have CSV and PDF export buttons. Click to download a copy of the data
  • Tabs — Many pages have tabs (like "Clients | New Client | Templates"). Click a tab to switch between views
  • Search Bar — Available on client and loan lists. Type a name or ID to filter results
  • Drilldown — On the Executive Dashboard, click any metric card to see the underlying loan details
  • Demo Mode — If you see "Demo Mode" at the top, you are viewing sample data. Real data entry is disabled in demo mode
💡 Tip — Tabs on Pages

Most pages in MFI Clarity have multiple tabs. For example, the Data Entry page has tabs: Clients · New Client · Templates · Loan · Workflow · Repayment · Upload · Find Docs · Settings. Always check all the tabs on a page — you may find what you need on a different tab.

Section 5 — Executive Dashboard

Your Institution's Health at a Glance

The Executive Dashboard is the first screen you see when you log in. It shows the most important numbers about your institution's loan portfolio in real-time. Admin Manager Board Director

📊 The 4 Main KPI Cards

  • Gross Portfolio — The total amount of money currently outstanding (owed to you) across all active loans. Shown in GHS.
  • Active Loans — The number of loans that are currently being repaid. This counts individual loan accounts, not clients.
  • PAR 30+ — "Portfolio at Risk" — the percentage of your portfolio where payments are more than 30 days overdue. A healthy MFI keeps this below 5%. If the number is red, it is above the warning threshold.
  • Provisions Required — How much money BOG requires you to set aside as a provision against potential loan losses, based on the classification of your loans.

📋 BOG Classification Table

Below the KPI cards is the BOG Classification Table. This shows all your loans grouped into 5 buckets:

  • Current — Payments up to date. Provision: 1%
  • OLEM — 1–30 days overdue. Provision: 5%
  • Substandard — 31–90 days overdue. Provision: 25%
  • Doubtful — 91–180 days overdue. Provision: 50%
  • Loss — Over 180 days overdue. Provision: 100%

Each row shows: number of loans in that bucket, total outstanding amount, provision rate %, and provision amount required.

💡 Tip — Using Drilldown

Click on any KPI card (Gross Portfolio, Active Loans, PAR 30+, or Provisions) to open a Drilldown Modal. This shows you a detailed list of every loan that makes up that number — including the client name, outstanding balance, and days overdue. Great for investigating problem accounts.

Section 6 — Managing Clients

Registering & Managing Borrowers

Go to Data Entry → New Client tab to register a new borrower. MFI Clarity supports four types of clients: Individual, Group, Cooperative, and SME. Admin Manager Field Officer

1

Select Client Type

At the top of the New Client form, choose: Individual (single person), Group (group lending), Cooperative, or SME (small business). The form changes slightly depending on what you choose. Most clients will be Individual.

2

Fill in Personal Details

  • First Name & Last Name — Client's full legal name as on ID
  • Date of Birth — Click the date field and select from the calendar
  • Gender — Select Male, Female, or Other
  • Nationality — Usually Ghanaian
  • Occupation — What the client does for a living (e.g., Trader, Farmer, Teacher)
3

Enter Identification Details (KYC)

  • Ghana Card Number — Must be in the format: GHA-XXXXXXXXX-X (e.g., GHA-123456789-0). The system will reject incorrect formats.
  • Ghana Card Expiry Date — The date on the card when it expires
  • Proof of Residence Type — Select the type of document used (Utility Bill, Tenancy Agreement, etc.)
4

Set Risk Category & Source of Funds

  • Risk Category — Select LOW, MEDIUM, or HIGH based on your institution's credit policy
  • Source of Funds — Where the client earns their income (e.g., Business Income, Salary, Farming)
5

Enter Location Details

  • Region — Select from the dropdown (all 16 Ghana regions)
  • District — The district list updates automatically based on the selected region
  • Town — The client's town or community
  • Landmark — A recognisable nearby location to help field officers find the client
  • GPS Address — The client's digital address (if known)
6

Enter Contact Information & Submit

Fill in Phone Number, Email (optional). For SME or Business clients, fill in Business Name, Business Type, Years in Business, Monthly Income, and Monthly Expenses. For Group clients, add each group member using the "Add Member" button. When all fields are complete, click Create Client.

💡 Tip — Searching for Existing Clients

Go to Data Entry → Clients tab. Use the search bar to find a client by name. You can also use the search when creating a loan — type a few letters of the client's name and matching results will appear in a dropdown.

Section 7 — Creating Loans

How to Create a New Loan

Go to Data Entry → Loan tab to create a new loan for an existing client. The client must already be registered in the system before you can create a loan for them. Admin Manager Field Officer

1

Select the Client

In the Client field, start typing the client's name. A dropdown list will show matching clients. Click on the correct client to select them. Their details will appear for confirmation.

2

Choose Loan Product / Category

Select the type of loan product from the dropdown list. Loan products are configured by your institution (e.g., Business Loan, Agricultural Loan, Emergency Loan). Each product may have different default interest rates and terms.

3

Enter Loan Financial Details

  • Principal Amount — The amount of money the client is borrowing (in GHS). Minimum: GHS 100. Maximum: GHS 5,000,000 (subject to your BOG tier limits)
  • Interest Rate (%) — The annual interest rate. Enter as a number (e.g., 24 for 24%)
  • Interest Method — FLAT: Interest is calculated on the original principal for the full term. REDUCING BALANCE: Interest is calculated on the outstanding balance — reduces as payments are made. Reducing Balance is more client-friendly and is the international standard.
  • Interest Calculation Frequency — How often interest is calculated: Daily, Weekly, Fortnightly, Monthly, Quarterly, or Annually
  • Term (Months) — How long the client has to repay (1 to 240 months)
  • Repayment Frequency — How often the client makes payments: Daily, Weekly, Fortnightly, or Monthly
  • Disbursement Date — The date the money was or will be given to the client
4

Enter Loan Purpose

Describe what the client intends to use the money for. This is required for KYC compliance. Example: "Purchase of trading stock for retail shop" or "Farm inputs for maize cultivation". Must be between 10 and 500 characters.

5

Configure Penalty Settings (Optional)

  • Grace Period — Number of days after a missed payment before penalties start (0–30 days)
  • Penalty Type — None, Flat Amount, % of Overdue Amount, % of Installment, or Daily Rate
  • Penalty Value — The amount or percentage to charge as penalty
6

Enter Collateral & Guarantor Details (if applicable)

  • Collateral Type — What asset the client is pledging (e.g., Land, Vehicle, Equipment)
  • Collateral Value — Estimated value of the collateral in GHS
  • Guarantor Name & Phone — A person who guarantees the loan
7

Review Affordability Check & Amortisation Schedule

The system will calculate the monthly payment amount and compare it to the client's income to perform an affordability check. Review the Amortisation Schedule — this table shows every payment the client needs to make, the date, the principal portion, the interest portion, and the remaining balance after each payment. Confirm it looks correct.

8

Submit the Loan

Click Create Loan. The loan will be saved and will appear in the client's profile and the portfolio. The loan will initially show as ACTIVE once disbursed.

⚠️ Important — BOG Loan Limit Check

MFI Clarity automatically enforces the loan limits set by the Bank of Ghana for your institution's tier. If you try to create a loan above your tier's maximum, the system will show an error. Contact your Admin to review your compliance tier in Settings if you believe the limits are incorrect.

💡 Tip — Bulk Import

If you have many clients or loans to add at once, go to Data Entry → Templates tab. Download the CSV template, fill in the data in Excel or Google Sheets, then upload the completed file. This is much faster than entering records one by one.

Section 8 — Recording Repayments

How to Post a Customer Repayment

When a client makes a loan payment at your counter or office, you must record it in MFI Clarity. Go to Data Entry → Repayment tab. Admin Manager Teller

1

Select the Client

Start typing the client's name in the Client field. Select the correct client from the dropdown. If the client has multiple active loans, a second dropdown will appear for loan selection.

2

Select the Loan

Choose the specific loan the client is paying for. The current outstanding balance and next installment due will be shown for your reference.

3

Enter Repayment Details

  • Amount — The exact amount the client is paying today (in GHS)
  • Payment Date — The date the payment is being made (defaults to today)
  • Payment Method — Cash, Mobile Money, or Bank Transfer
  • Reference Number — If payment is by Mobile Money or bank transfer, enter the transaction reference number (e.g., GHA-XXXXXXXX)
  • Notes — Any additional notes about this payment (optional)
4

Submit the Repayment

Click Post Repayment. The system immediately updates the loan's outstanding balance. If the payment clears the outstanding amount, the loan status changes to REPAID. A receipt can be printed from the confirmation screen.

💡 Tip — Partial Payments

You can post any amount — even less than the full installment. The system will allocate the payment first to interest, then to principal, per standard accounting practice. The outstanding balance will update accordingly.

Section 9 — Field Operations

Recording Collections in the Field (with GPS)

Field Officers use this module to record loan payments collected outside the office — at a client's home, market, or farm. Go to Field Operations in the sidebar. Admin Manager Field Officer

1

Go to Field Operations → New Collection

Click on Field Operations in the sidebar, then click the New Collection tab at the top of the page.

2

Select Client and Loan

Choose the client from the dropdown. Once a client is selected, their active loans will appear in the Loan dropdown. Select the loan being paid.

3

Enter Collection Amount and Method

  • Amount Collected — How much cash or mobile money you received from the client
  • Collection Method — Cash, Mobile Money, or Bank Transfer
  • Mobile Money Reference — If paid via Mobile Money, enter the transaction code
  • Notes — Any special notes (client refused to pay, partial reason, etc.)
4

Capture GPS Location (Important)

Click the 📍 Get My Location button. Allow the browser to access your device's location. The system will capture your GPS coordinates (latitude, longitude) and accuracy reading. This proves you were physically at the collection site and protects you from disputes.

5

Upload Receipt Photo & Capture Signature (Recommended)

Take a photo of the receipt or any payment proof using your phone camera and upload it. Optionally, capture the client's digital signature on your phone screen as confirmation they received the service.

6

Submit — Collection Goes to Pending Queue

Click Submit Collection. The collection is saved with status PENDING. It will appear in the Collections Queue tab for your supervisor (Manager or Admin) to review and approve. Once approved, it automatically updates the loan balance.

ℹ️ Collection Status Flow

PENDING → Field officer submitted, waiting for supervisor review  |  VERIFIED → Supervisor has confirmed the collection  |  APPROVED → Collection is fully approved and the loan balance has been updated

Section 11 — Repayment Trends

Viewing 60-Day Collection Performance

Go to Repayments in the sidebar. This page shows your institution's collection performance over the last 60 days. Admin Manager

Total Collected

Total GHS amount collected across all loans in the last 60 days.

Total Payments

The number of individual payment transactions posted in the period.

Average Daily Collection

Total collected divided by 60 days — your average daily inflow.

The area chart shows daily collections as a visual graph — peaks show days with high collections, dips show slow days. The table below the chart gives the exact figure for each date. Export as CSV or PDF for management meetings.

Section 12 — Management Dashboard

Daily Operational Control for Managers

Go to Management in the sidebar. This dashboard is designed for Branch Managers and Operations Managers who need to monitor day-to-day performance. Admin Manager

📊 Dashboard Tabs

  • Daily Operations — Today's summary KPIs and operational metrics. Activity log of recent transactions.
  • Collections — Collections trend chart (Daily/Weekly/Monthly). Collection performance metrics showing how much has been recovered vs. what was expected.
  • Disbursements — New loans disbursed in the period. Disbursement trends chart. Upcoming disbursement schedule.

  • Monthly Reporting — Switch the period selector to "Monthly" for month-end management reporting
  • Arrears Meeting — Use the Arrears Tracker every week to identify which accounts need follow-up and measure recovery performance

Section 13 — Board of Directors Dashboard

Strategic Governance for Board Members

Go to Board in the sidebar. This dashboard is designed for non-executive board directors, chairpersons, and senior executives who need strategic oversight. Admin Manager Board Director

1

Select the Reporting Period

At the top of the page, choose Weekly, Monthly, or Quarterly. For Monthly and Quarterly, select the specific period from the dropdown. All charts and numbers on the page will update to reflect the chosen period.

2

Executive Summary Tab

Shows the 4 headline KPIs (Portfolio, Active Loans, PAR 30+, Provisions) with trend arrows showing whether each metric is improving or worsening compared to the previous period. Compliance badges show whether your institution is Compliant, on Watch, or Non-Compliant with BOG requirements.

3

Strategic KPIs Tab

Shows growth metrics (portfolio growth rate, new loan disbursements), quality metrics (PAR trends, loan loss rates), and efficiency metrics (cost per loan, staff productivity). Helps the board evaluate whether the institution is growing sustainably.

4

Risk Analysis Tab

Shows risk stratification (how much of the portfolio is at various risk levels), concentration risk (are you too exposed to one sector or geography?), and geographic risk spread across regions.

5

Trends & Peer Panel Tab

Shows quarterly trends over multiple periods so the board can see long-term patterns. Peer comparison data benchmarks your institution's performance against sector averages.

ℹ️ Board Director Access

Board Directors have read-only access. They can see all data and click drilldowns to examine underlying loans, but they cannot create, edit, or delete any records. This ensures clean separation between governance and operational management.

Section 14 — Shareholder / Investor Portal

Managing Investors and Tracking Returns

Go to Shareholders in the sidebar. This portal manages your institution's equity investors and gives shareholders real-time visibility into their investments. Admin Manager Shareholder (own data only)

📈 Key Investor Metrics (Top of Page)

  • Total Investment — Sum of all capital contributed by shareholders
  • Current Value — What the investment is worth today (with ROI %)
  • Total Dividends Earned — All dividends paid to date (with any pending amount shown)
  • Unit Value — Current GHS value of one share unit

📊 Charts

  • Portfolio Allocation Pie Chart — Shows how investor capital is split between active loans (Loaned Out) and cash held (In Reserve). Investors can see their money is working.
  • Share Unit Performance Line Chart — Shows how the value of one share unit has grown over time. Based at 100 at the start for easy percentage reading.
1

Add a New Shareholder (Admin/Manager)

Click the Add Shareholder button. Fill in: Full Name, Email, Phone, and Initial Investment Amount. The system will create the shareholder record and record their first investment transaction.

2

Record a Transaction

Click Record Transaction. Select the shareholder, transaction type (INVESTMENT = add more shares, DIVIDEND_REINVEST = convert dividend to shares, WITHDRAWAL = cash out), share units, and unit value. The transaction is permanently recorded.

3

Process a Dividend

Click Process Dividend. Set the dividend rate (%). The system automatically calculates each shareholder's payout based on their units. Set the payout date and click confirm. Each payout is recorded and marked as PAID or PENDING.

Section 15 — Departmental Reports

Accounts, Collections & Credit Department Analytics

Go to Departments in the sidebar. This page has three main tabs covering three departments. Use the period selector (Daily / Weekly / Monthly / Quarterly) at the top. Admin Manager

📒 Accounts Tab

  • GL Summary — Total Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Income, Expenses, Net Income
  • Trial Balance — Full trial balance table showing Account Code, Name, Type, Debit Balance, and Credit Balance. A status badge shows if the balance is Balanced or Unbalanced.
  • Export the trial balance to CSV for your accountant

💼 Collections Tab

  • Recovery Metrics — Opening Arrears, Recovered (green), New Arrears (red), Closing Arrears, Recovery Rate %
  • Collector Performance Table — Each field officer/collector's performance: Arrears Assigned, Amount Collected, Collection Rate %, Clients Visited, Accounts Cured
  • Helps managers identify top performers and those needing support

🎓 Credit Tab

  • Approval Metrics — Applications Received, Approved (green), Rejected (red), Approval Rate %, Amount Disbursed, Average TAT (days to process)
  • Loan Application Pipeline — Visual flow of applications through approval stages
  • Rejection Analysis — Charts showing why loans were rejected
  • Credit Officer Scorecard — Each officer graded A+ to D on: Approval Rate (20%), Target Achievement (30%), Quality Score (30%), Efficiency (20%)
  • Product Performance — Which loan products have highest PAR
💡 Tip — Monthly Management Meetings

Run the Departmental Reports at the end of each month on the "Monthly" period setting. Export each tab (Accounts, Collections, Credit) to PDF. Use these exports as your standardised management pack for board and management meetings — consistent, professional, and always current.

Section 16 — Financial Reports

Full Financial Statements & Profitability Metrics

Go to Financial Reports in the sidebar. This is your institution's complete financial reporting hub. Admin Manager Board Director

📑 Financial Statements Tab

Three core statements:

  • Balance Sheet — Assets (loans, cash, property) vs. Liabilities (deposits, borrowings) vs. Equity (shareholders' funds)
  • Income Statement — Interest income, fee income, operating expenses, loan loss provisions, and net income
  • Cash Flow Statement — Where cash came from and where it went in the period

📊 Profitability Metrics Tab

Key international microfinance performance indicators:

  • NIM — Net Interest Margin: income earned on loans vs. cost of funds
  • OSS — Operational Self-Sufficiency: are revenues covering all costs? (Target: above 100%)
  • ROA — Return on Assets: profit as % of total assets
  • ROE — Return on Equity: profit as % of shareholders' funds
  • Cost-to-Income — Operating costs as % of income (lower is better)

Each metric shows a status indicator: green (good), yellow (watch), red (critical).

⚠️ PAR Analysis Tab

Detailed Portfolio at Risk analysis. PAR aging breakdown (PAR 1, PAR 7, PAR 30, PAR 90, PAR 180). Trend line showing whether PAR is improving or worsening. Portfolio quality metrics.

🏦 Deposits Monitoring Tab

Deposit mobilisation trends over time. Deposit composition by type. Deposit stability metrics (volatile vs. stable deposits). Essential for institutions that take deposits from the public.

Section 17 — Regulatory Reports (BOG)

Bank of Ghana Compliance Reporting

Go to Regulatory Reports in the sidebar. These reports are required by the Bank of Ghana. MFI Clarity generates them automatically from your live data. Admin Manager Board Director

📊 CAR Calculator Tab

Calculates your Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR) in real-time. Shows core capital, supplementary capital, risk-weighted assets, and your current CAR vs. the BOG minimum (10%). A green/red badge shows if you are compliant.

💧 Liquidity Ratio Tab

Shows your Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) and Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR). Compares them to BOG minimums for your tier. Essential for passing liquidity examinations.

📋 Classification Tab

Full loan classification table (same as Executive Dashboard). Shows all loans in Current/OLEM/Substandard/Doubtful/Loss buckets with provision calculations. Print or export for BOG submission.

📄 Prudential Returns Tab

All BOG-mandated prudential return forms. Status tracking shows which returns are due, submitted, or pending. Helps your compliance team stay on top of reporting deadlines.

🔍 AML Reports Tab

Anti-Money Laundering reports per the AML/CFT&P Act, 2022. Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) history. Customer Due Diligence summary. Helps you demonstrate AML compliance to BOG and FIC during examinations.

Section 18 — Audit Log

Complete Activity Trail

Go to Audit Log in the sidebar. Every action in MFI Clarity is recorded here automatically — you cannot delete or edit audit records. Admin Manager

The audit log records: who did the action (user name and email), what action they took (created loan, edited client, posted repayment, approved collection, logged in/out), and exactly when it happened (date and time). Use the search bar to find specific events. Filter by date range, action type, or user. During a BOG examination, examiners can review this log to verify that operations were conducted correctly.

Section 19 — User Management

Managing Staff Accounts & Roles

Go to User Management in the sidebar. Only Admins can manage users. Admin only

👥 Staff Roles Tab

View all users in your organisation. Assign or change roles: ADMIN, MANAGER, FIELD_OFFICER, TELLER, BOARD_DIRECTOR. To assign a role, find the user, click the role dropdown, select the new role, and save. To remove a user's access, set their role to inactive or remove their role assignment.

📋 Client Assignments Tab

Assign specific clients to specific credit officers or field officers. This ensures each field officer can only see and manage their assigned clients (enforcing data privacy). You can reassign clients between officers here, or do bulk assignments for a new officer joining.

⚠️ Important — Adding New Staff

New staff must first register their own account (using the /register page) with their work email. Once they have registered and verified their email, the Admin goes to User Management and assigns them the correct role. The new user will then see the menus appropriate for their role when they log in.

Section 20 & 21 — Settings & Troubleshooting

⚙️ Settings (/settings) — Admin Only

  • Organisation Tab — Update your legal name, trading name, address, phone, email, website, registration number, and TIN at any time
  • Compliance Tab — Set your BOG Tier: Tier 1 (Rural Banks), Tier 2 (Savings & Loans), Tier 3 (Finance Houses), Tier 4 (MFIs), MFC (Micro Finance Companies). Your tier determines loan limits and CAR requirements enforced by the system

❓ Troubleshooting & FAQ

  • Cannot log in? — Check your email and password. Use "Forgot Password" to reset. Make sure your email was verified.
  • Data not loading? — Check your internet connection. Try refreshing the page (F5). Clear your browser cache if the problem persists.
  • Cannot see a menu? — Your role may not have access. Ask your Admin to check your role assignment.
  • Error when creating a loan? — Check that the principal amount does not exceed your BOG tier limit. Make sure the client exists and is active.
  • GPS not working in field? — Allow location access in your browser when prompted. You must be outdoors for best GPS accuracy. If accuracy is poor, try moving to an open area.
  • Ghana Card number rejected? — Must be in format GHA-XXXXXXXXX-X exactly (GHA, hyphen, 9 digits, hyphen, 1 digit).
