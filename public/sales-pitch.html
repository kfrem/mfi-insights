The Reality Right Now

Sound Familiar? Every MFI in Ghana Faces This.

Microfinance institutions, rural banks, and credit unions lose millions in operational inefficiency, regulatory penalties, and portfolio leakage — not because of bad intentions, but because of outdated tools. These are the six critical problems costing Ghana's financial sector real money right now.

✗

Manual Prudential Returns

Staff spend days compiling data from Excel sheets, paper records, and disparate systems to meet BoG reporting deadlines. One error means regulatory scrutiny and potential fines.

✗

No Real-Time Portfolio Visibility

Management can't see PAR ratios, collection rates, or disbursement volumes in real time. Decisions are made on week-old data. By the time you spot a problem, it's already a crisis.

✗

Field Officers With No Digital Tools

Collections happen in cash with paper receipts. GPS verification is nonexistent. Fraud risk is high. Reconciliation takes days. And when the network goes down, work stops completely.

✗

CAR & Liquidity Non-Compliance

Capital Adequacy Ratios and liquidity requirements are calculated manually — often incorrectly. Institutions learn they're non-compliant only when BoG examines them.

✗

No Audit Trail

Who approved that loan? Who modified that client record? When BoG or internal auditors ask, the answer is often "we don't know." This exposes the institution to fraud and regulatory action.

✗

Shareholders Left in the Dark

Investors and board directors have no real-time visibility into the institution's health. AGM presentations are based on stale data. Dividend calculations are manual and error-prone.

Security & Architecture

Enterprise-Grade Security That Regulators Demand

MFI Clarity is built on the same security foundations used by global banks. Every layer — from database to UI — is hardened against unauthorised access, data leakage, and compliance violations.

🔐 Row-Level Security (RLS)

Every database query is filtered at the PostgreSQL level. A user in Organisation A can never see Organisation B's data — even if the application code has a bug. This is not application-level filtering; it's database-enforced isolation.

👤 Role-Based Access Control

Five distinct roles — Admin, Manager, Field Officer, Teller, Board Director — each with precisely scoped permissions. A Teller cannot approve loans. A Field Officer cannot modify client KYC. Role enforcement happens at the API level.

🏢 Multi-Tenant Architecture

Each institution operates in complete isolation. The org_id permeates every table, every query, every policy. One platform, hundreds of institutions, zero data crossover. Perfect for apex bodies managing multiple MFIs.

📝 Immutable Audit Logging

Every financial transaction, every CRUD operation, every login is recorded with timestamps, user IDs, IP addresses, and before/after values. Audit logs cannot be modified or deleted by any user role.
